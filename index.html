HERE ARE THE REAL ANSWERS.

Most homeowners don't notice internal rot until a limb fails — sometimes years after it starts. Signs include soft or discolored wood at the base, fungal growth, dead branches in the crown, and hollow-sounding trunks when knocked. The safest move is a free inspection from a certified arborist. We'll evaluate the tree and tell you exactly what we find, no pressure.

If a tree falls on a covered structure — your home, fence, or outbuilding — most homeowner policies will cover removal up to a set limit. We work directly with adjusters every week and know how to document damage properly. We'll walk you through the entire process so you're not navigating it alone.

Most standard stumps are ground down and cleaned up in under an hour. Larger stumps or multiple stumps may take a half day. We grind deep enough to allow for replanting or sod — usually 8–12 inches below grade — and haul off all debris before we leave.

For most Texas homeowners, absolutely. Improper pruning can permanently damage a tree's structure, invite disease, and create liability hazards. A certified arborist prunes to ISA standards — removing the right wood at the right cuts to promote healthy growth and reduce long-term risk.
